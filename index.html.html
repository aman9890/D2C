Diamond Clarity Grades

Clarity measures the microscopic birthmarks—inclusions (internal) and blemishes (external)—within a stone. Lower clarity scores represent denser microscopic carbon marks.

Clarity Level: FL (Flawless)
SI1 VS2 VVS1 FL

Flawless diamonds show absolutely zero inclusions under 10x magnification. Extremely rare.

Diamond Coloration Grades

Truly luxury diamonds are chemically pure and structurally perfect, showing absolutely zero tint. The scale runs from D (completely colorless) to Z (light yellow/brown tint).

Color Spectrum: D (Colorless)
D F H J

D is the highest possible grade. A stone of absolute purity, refracting perfectly clear white light.

The Precision Cut

Cut is widely considered the most vital of the 4Cs. It refers not to diamond shape, but how expertly its facets interact with light. An ideal cut returns maximum brilliance back through the top.

Understanding Carat Weight

Carat is the measurement of a diamond's physical weight. One metric carat equals precisely 200 milligrams. Even a fractional increase in carat size escalates rarity exponentially.

Interactive Carat weight: 2.00 Carats
0.5ct 1.0ct 1.5ct 2.0ct 2.5ct 3.0ct

A 2-carat stone is exponentially rarer than two 1-carat stones combined, making its valuation significantly higher.
